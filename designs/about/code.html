The Philosophy

This is not a directory. It’s a diary of caffeine-induced revelations across Berlin. In a city where every street corner claims to have the "best" light roast, I seek the truth in the bottom of a 30ml cup.

Coffee is subjective. It’s a collision of chemistry, weather, and whether or not the person behind the counter looked at you with mild Berlin-flavored disdain.

The Attributes

To keep things plain, I use a purely typographic system to categorize the taste profile. No icons, no color-coding—just the facts.

(+ POSITIVE)

Attributes wrapped in parentheses with a plus indicate qualities that define the excellence of the cup. (e.g., (+ SILKY)).

(- NEGATIVE)

Attributes with a minus are the detractors—the notes that pulled the score down. (e.g., (- BITTER)).

[CONTEXT]

Brackets denote roast style or specific bean origins that set the stage.

The Variables

An 8.9 in the Mitte sunshine is a 7.2 in a Neukölln downpour. My reviews always account for:

  • Weather Temperature and humidity change how we perceive acidity. Fog calls for body; sun calls for citrus.
  • Mood If I'm Melancholic, I want a heavy, comforting chocolate. If I'm Focused, give me that sharp, electric fruit.
